Lion's mane

Description

This beautiful species, appearing as a white waterfall of cascading icicles, is found on broad leaf trees and logs. The subject of recent studies, Lion's Mane (Hericium erinaceus) is renowned for providing support to the brain and nervous system*.

Constituents: polysaccharides (beta glucans, arabinoxylane, glucose, xylose, galactose and mannose), glycoproteins, ergosterols, triterpenoids and other myco-nutrients,which are essential for Supporting Natural Immunity*.

  • Provides cerebral and nervous system support*

Cultivation

  1. Prepare agar on petri dishes
  2. Inoculate petri dishes with spores in sterile environment
  3. Sterilize substrate, allow to cool
  4. Inoculate substrate with bites of mycelium grown on agar
  5. Allow mycelium to grow through the substrate
  6. Prepare the spawn, mix with the substrate
  7. Let the mix sit and wait for the beutiful fungi to pop :)
